# History of the Internet

## Executive Summary
The history of the internet is a story of rapid evolution and growth. From its humble beginnings as a research network to its current status as a global commercial phenomenon, the internet has undergone significant transformations over the years. This report highlights key milestones in the development of packet switching, the role of IP in expansion and standardization, and major events in the transition from academic and government networks to global commercial use.

## Sub-question: How did the development of packet switching contribute to the creation of the internet?

The development of packet switching was a crucial contribution to the creation of the internet. The concept of packet switching was first proposed by Paul Baran in 1964 as part of his work on the US Department of Defense's ARPANET project [1]. Packet switching allows multiple devices to share the same communication channel, making efficient use of bandwidth [2], enables data to be reassembled at its destination even if some packets are lost or corrupted during transmission [3], and allows networks to grow and expand easily [4].

The development of packet switching also led to the creation of various network protocols, such as TCP/IP, which is still widely used today [5]. The use of packet switching has enabled a wide range of applications and services on the internet, including email, file transfer, and online gaming.

## Sub-question: What role did the Internet Protocol (IP) play in the expansion and standardization of the internet?

The Internet Protocol (IP) played a crucial role in the expansion and standardization of the internet. IP was developed by Vint Cerf and Bob Kahn in the 1970s as part of the ARPANET project [6]. It enabled the creation of a global network by providing a standardized way for devices to communicate with each other using IP addresses [7]. This allowed data packets to be routed efficiently across the network, and it enabled the growth of the internet into a global system.

The use of IP addresses has enabled the growth of the internet into a global system, with billions of devices connected to the network. The standardization of IP has also facilitated the development of new technologies, such as mobile broadband and IoT devices, which rely on IP for communication.

## Sub-question: What are some key milestones in the expansion of the internet beyond academic and government networks to global commercial use?

Key milestones in the expansion of the internet beyond academic and government networks to global commercial use include:

1.  **ARPANET's Expansion**: The precursor to the Internet, ARPANET, was initially a research network developed by the US Department of Defense in the late 1960s [8]. It expanded rapidly and began to serve other fields beyond education and research.
2.  **Establishment of Internet Service Providers (ISPs)**: As the internet grew, commercial ISPs emerged to provide access to the network [9]. These ISPs connected users to the global network and enabled widespread adoption.
3.  **Development of Network Access Points (NAPs)**: To facilitate connectivity between different ISPs, NAPs were established in the US [10]. These high-level access points allowed for the exchange of traffic between networks.
4.  **Global Expansion**: The internet expanded globally as more countries and regions gained access to the network [11]. This expansion was driven by advances in technology, economic growth, and increased demand for online services.

## Conclusion
The history of the internet is a story of rapid evolution and growth. From its humble beginnings as a research network to its current status as a global commercial phenomenon, the internet has undergone significant transformations over the years. The development of packet switching, the role of IP in expansion and standardization, and major events in the transition from academic and government networks to global commercial use are all crucial milestones in this story.
